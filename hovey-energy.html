TCPA Compliance Architecture

Built In — Not an Afterthought
Bottom line on B2B AI calling: AI voice calling for B2B energy sales is legal and workable. The FCC (Feb 2024) confirmed AI-generated voices are treated the same as robocalls under TCPA. B2B calls to business landlines enjoy significant exemptions — but cell phones, sole proprietors, and state "mini-TCPA" laws introduce landmines. DTN builds every required protection into Phase 1 of the engagement.
Immediate AI Disclosure
Every call opens with AI identity disclosure in the first sentence. This is non-negotiable under FCC guidance and built directly into the agent's opening prompt. Example: "Hi, this is an AI calling on behalf of Hovey Energy…" It's also a differentiator — transparency builds trust faster than pretending.
DNC Scrub Before Every Dial
National DNC Registry scrub is integrated into the dialing workflow. No number is dialed without real-time verification. The National DNC Registry includes individual business numbers for sole proprietors — these are not exempt and must be scrubbed.
Real-Time Opt-Out Handling
The AI agent recognizes opt-out language ("take me off your list," "do not call," "remove me") and triggers immediate suppression. Opt-out requests are logged, and suppression takes effect within 24 hours. DTN tests this regularly as part of monthly compliance monitoring.
B2B Call Flow Design
Call targeting is designed for business landlines, not cell phones. Cell phone numbers for business contacts require documented consent regardless of B2B context. DTN's lead list design and calling protocols are structured to maintain the B2B exemption wherever applicable.
Four-Year Call Log Retention
Every call attempt, consent record, and opt-out event is logged and retained for a minimum of four years. ElevenLabs records and transcribes every call automatically. DTN maintains a structured compliance archive accessible for any regulatory review.
Counsel Review Checkpoint
Before go-live, DTN flags the full script and call flow for telecom compliance review by qualified counsel. This is built into Week 1 of the Discovery phase — not an optional add-on. TCPA violations start at $500/call and reach $1,500 for willful violations. Counsel review is a fixed cost that pays for itself on the first avoided violation.
