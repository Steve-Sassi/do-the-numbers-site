DTN Consulting Part One: Why This Moment Matters
Part One

Why This Moment Matters

The Shift Is Already Happening

Your competitors' sales teams are already experimenting with AI. Some of them are doing it well. Most of them are doing it wrong — pasting prompts into ChatGPT and calling it a strategy. But a small percentage are vibe coding in plain language and building real workflows that are changing how they prospect, prepare, follow up, and close.

That gap is going to widen fast.

The question for every sales leader right now is not whether to embrace AI. That decision has already been made by the market. The question is: will your team be in front of this, or scrambling to catch up?

What AI Actually Does for a Sales Team

Let's be precise, because this is where most of the confusion lives.

AI does not replace salespeople. It does not make calls, build relationships, or earn trust. What it does is eliminate the administrative burden that keeps your best reps from doing the things only humans can do.

Think about where your reps' time actually goes in a given week:

  • Writing follow-up emails after meetings
  • Researching prospects before calls
  • Updating CRM records
  • Preparing for QBRs and pipeline reviews
  • Building custom decks and proposals
  • Summarizing calls and sending recaps

These tasks are necessary. They are also not selling. AI can handle most of them — faster, more consistently, and without draining the energy your reps need to close.

The result is not a smaller team. It's a more effective one. We think of it as bringing back the dedicated executive assistant — except this one works around the clock, never forgets a detail, and integrates directly into the tools your team already uses.

DTN Consulting Part Two: The Five AI Use Cases
Part Two

The Five AI Use Cases
That Are Changing Sales

These are the highest-impact areas where enterprise sales teams are deploying AI right now, listed in order of ease of implementation.

Use Case 01

AI-Assisted Prospect Research

The Problem

Reps spend 30–60 minutes researching a prospect before a first call — pulling from LinkedIn, company websites, news, and CRM. The result is inconsistent and time-consuming.

The AI Solution

A well-designed AI workflow generates a comprehensive prospect brief in under two minutes — covering recent news, background, pain points, and talking points aligned to your solution.

The Result

Reps walk into every call more prepared. Call quality improves. Conversion from first call to second meeting increases immediately.

Use Case 02

AI-Powered Follow-Up and Email Drafting

The Problem

Post-meeting follow-up is critical — and one of the most inconsistently executed touchpoints. Some reps send detailed follow-ups in an hour. Others send generic notes two days later.

The AI Solution

An AI workflow triggered after a meeting drafts a personalized follow-up referencing specific conversation points, next steps, and relevant resources. The rep reviews and sends in minutes.

The Result

Follow-up quality becomes consistent across the entire team. Speed to follow-up improves dramatically. Prospects receive a more professional, attentive experience every time.

Use Case 03

AI-Driven Pipeline Management and Forecasting

The Problem

CRM hygiene is the bane of every sales leader's existence. Reps don't update records consistently. Forecast accuracy suffers. Deal reviews become exercises in data archaeology.

The AI Solution

AI workflows listen to call recordings, read email threads, and automatically update CRM records — flagging deals that have gone quiet and surfacing next steps that haven't been actioned.

The Result

CRM data stays current without manual rep effort. Forecast accuracy improves. Sales leaders spend deal reviews on strategy, not data cleanup.

Use Case 04

AI-Assisted Proposal and Deck Creation

The Problem

Building custom proposals is one of the most time-intensive tasks in enterprise sales. A rep might spend 4–8 hours building a proposal for a single deal.

The AI Solution

AI workflows take a deal brief as input and generate a first-draft proposal or deck — pre-populated with case studies, customized messaging, and a financial model. The rep refines rather than builds from scratch.

The Result

Proposal creation drops from hours to minutes. Quality and consistency improve. Reps respond to RFPs faster and pursue more opportunities simultaneously.

Use Case 05

Custom AI Agents Integrated Into Your Sales Stack

The Problem

Most sales teams use Salesforce or HubSpot, Outreach or Salesloft, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Slack, and more. These tools don't talk to each other intelligently. Data sits in silos. Context is lost.

The AI Solution

Custom AI agents built across your existing stack — connecting your CRM, communication tools, calendar, and data sources — give your reps a single intelligent interface that surfaces the right information at the right moment.

The Result

This is the executive assistant model at scale. The agent handles the orchestration. The rep handles the relationship. This is the use case that transforms the entire workflow.

DTN Consulting Part Three: The Triple A Approach
Part Three

The DTN Triple A Approach

Every engagement we run — whether it's a one-day workshop or a full custom implementation — follows the same three-part framework.

A

Aim

Before we touch a single tool, we sit down with your leadership team and define success in measurable terms. Which part of your sales process has the most friction? What would a 20% reduction in admin time mean for your pipeline? The Aim phase ensures we're solving the right problem — not just implementing technology for its own sake.

A

Architecture

There is no universal AI sales solution. We design a solution that fits your team — your tools, your process, your culture, your compliance requirements. We select the right AI models, build the right integrations, and create the right experience for your reps. We come to this as sales practitioners, not technologists — we know what reps will actually use.

A

Accountability

The most common failure in AI adoption isn't the technology. It's the change management. Teams get excited in the kickoff, struggle through the learning curve, and quietly revert to old habits within 90 days. We don't let that happen. We set targets, track them weekly, and hold ourselves — and your team — to them.

Part Four

AI Adoption Without the Risk

We hear the same concerns from sales leaders across every industry. They're valid. Here's how we address them.

"I'm worried about data security and compliance."

Every workflow we design is built with your data governance requirements in mind. We don't connect tools that shouldn't be connected, we don't store sensitive data in unsecured environments, and we work within your existing IT and legal frameworks. If you're in a regulated industry, we've worked in regulated environments.

"My reps won't adopt new tools."

The reason reps don't adopt new tools is usually one of two things: the tool doesn't actually make their job easier, or they weren't trained in a way that made it click. We solve both. Every workflow we build is tested with actual reps before rollout, and our training is built by salespeople — not instructional designers who've never run a pipeline review.

"We tried AI before and it didn't work."

Usually, what happened was that someone subscribed to a tool, turned it on, and hoped for the best. AI implementation requires intentional design. The tool is 20% of the outcome. The workflow, the training, and the accountability are the other 80%. That's what we provide.

"How do we know if it's working?"

We establish baseline metrics before we start and track against them throughout the engagement — time-to-follow-up, call prep time, CRM update frequency, pipeline coverage ratio. You'll know it's working because you'll see it in the numbers.
